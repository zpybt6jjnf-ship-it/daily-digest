Macro Trends

Data Center Power Demands Reshape Grid Governance. The bipartisan alliance between the Trump administration and 13 governors to pressure PJM on data center costs signals a fundamental shift in electricity market politics. With data centers accounting for 49% of PJM's capacity procurement costs ($23.1 billion of $47.2 billion total), traditional rate structures are breaking down. The push for "bring your own generation" and emergency auctions reflects growing political consensus that tech companies must internalize their grid impacts rather than socializing costs to residential ratepayers.

Offshore Wind Survives Legal Challenges, But Policy Uncertainty Persists. Three consecutive court victories for offshore wind developers demonstrate the limits of executive action against projects with vested property rights and advanced construction. However, the administration's willingness to suspend active projects, combined with the SPEED Act's offshore wind exclusion, signals continued policy headwinds for the sector. Developers with projects in earlier stages face greater uncertainty than those nearing completion.

Nuclear Renaissance Accelerates Through Corporate Procurement. Meta's 6.6 GW nuclear agreements, combined with New York's expanded 5 GW target and DOE's $2.7 billion uranium enrichment investment, indicate nuclear is moving from policy aspiration to commercial reality. The Vistra-Meta uprates alone will add 433 MW of new nuclear capacity—a significant departure from decades of fleet shrinkage. However, the decade-plus timeline for most new capacity underscores the gap between announcements and actual grid impacts.
